Gait variability CoV reaches stable estimates within 10–15 strides in movement disorder groups, suggesting short-distance clinical walks are sufficient for assessment in these populations.

Evidence:
[[kroneberg2019LessMore]] found that Pearson correlation R > 0.8 between cumulative CoV and the 40-stride reference was reached by the 10th stride for ATX and PD groups, and by the 16th stride for ET. In healthy older adults the threshold was not reached until the 16th–20th stride, and our own analysis suggests 25 strides may be needed in healthy populations. The finding may be more robust in groups with elevated pathological variability, where the signal-to-noise ratio is higher and fewer strides are needed to capture the underlying variability pattern. Contrasts with: [[Reliable CoV estimation requires at least 25 strides in healthy older adults]].
